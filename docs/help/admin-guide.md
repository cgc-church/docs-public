# Church Administrator Guide

Welcome to the Christ Gospel Church administrator guide. This page covers everything you need to know to manage your church's content, users, and settings through the CGC Admin Dashboard.

## Accessing the Admin Dashboard

The Admin Dashboard is available at [admin.christgospel.org](https://admin.christgospel.org).

### How to sign in

1. Visit [admin.christgospel.org](https://admin.christgospel.org)
2. Enter your **email address** and **password**
3. Click **Sign In**
4. If you have two-factor authentication enabled, enter your verification code when prompted
5. You will be taken to the dashboard home screen

::: info
Admin accounts are created by your church's existing administrators. If you need admin access, contact your church admin team or email **support@christgospel.org**.
:::

### First-time setup

When you first receive your admin invitation:

1. Check your email for the invitation from CGC
2. Click the link in the email to set your password
3. Sign in at [admin.christgospel.org](https://admin.christgospel.org)
4. You may be prompted to set up **two-factor authentication** for added security (strongly recommended)
5. Complete your profile by adding your name and any other required details

## Dashboard Overview

After signing in, you will see the main dashboard with a navigation sidebar on the left. Here is a quick overview of the main sections:

- **Home** — A summary view with quick stats, recent activity, and shortcuts to common tasks

## Managing Sermons

Sermons are the core of the CGC platform. As an administrator, you can upload, edit, schedule, and organize sermons for your congregation.

### Uploading a new sermon

1. Go to **Sermons** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add Sermon** (or the **+** button)
3. Fill in the sermon details:
   - **Title** — The name of the sermon
   - **Preacher** — Select the preacher from the dropdown, or add a new one
   - **Date** — The date the sermon was preached
   - **Topic / Category** — Assign one or more topics to help users find the sermon
   - **Scripture Reference** — Add any relevant Bible references
   - **Description** — A brief summary of the sermon content
4. Upload the **audio file** (MP3 format recommended)
5. Optionally upload a **video file** (MP4 format recommended)
6. Click **Save** or **Publish**

### Editing a sermon

1. Go to **Sermons** and find the sermon you want to edit
2. Click on the sermon title or the **Edit** button
3. Make your changes to any of the fields
4. Click **Save** to apply changes

### Scheduling a sermon for future publication

1. While creating or editing a sermon, look for the **Publish Date** field
2. Set the date and time when you want the sermon to become available
3. Click **Schedule** — the sermon will automatically go live at the scheduled time
4. Scheduled sermons are marked with a clock icon in the sermon list

### Unpublishing or deleting a sermon

- To **unpublish**: Edit the sermon and change its status to **Draft**. It will be hidden from users but not deleted.
- To **delete**: Click the **Delete** button on the sermon. You will be asked to confirm. Deleted sermons cannot be recovered.

::: tip
If you are unsure whether to delete a sermon, unpublish it first. You can always re-publish it later.
:::

## Managing Users

The Users section allows you to view all registered users, manage roles, and invite new administrators.

### Viewing users

1. Go to **Users** in the sidebar
2. You will see a list of all registered users
3. Use the **search bar** to find a specific user by name or email
4. Click on a user to view their profile, subscription status, and activity

### User roles and permissions

The CGC platform has the following roles:

| Role | Description |
|---|---|
| **User** | Standard member. Can browse content, stream, create playlists, and manage their own account |
| **Subscriber** | A user with an active subscription. Has access to premium features like offline downloads |
| **Admin** | Full access to the Admin Dashboard. Can manage content, users, and settings |
| **Super Admin** | All admin privileges plus the ability to manage other admin accounts and access sensitive settings |

### Inviting a new administrator

1. Go to **Users** in the sidebar
2. Click **Invite Admin**
3. Enter the person's **email address**
4. Select the **role** you want to assign (Admin or Super Admin)
5. Click **Send Invitation**
6. The person will receive an email with instructions to set up their account

### Changing a user's role

1. Go to **Users** and find the user
2. Click on their profile
3. Click **Edit Role**
4. Select the new role from the dropdown
5. Click **Save**

::: warning
Be careful when granting Admin or Super Admin roles. Only trusted team members should have elevated access.
:::

## Managing Music

The Music section lets you manage songs, albums, artists, and genres available on the platform.

### Adding a song

1. Go to **Music** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add Song**
3. Fill in the song details:
   - **Title** — The name of the song
   - **Artist** — Select an existing artist or create a new one
   - **Album** — Assign the song to an album (optional)
   - **Genre** — Select the genre
   - **Duration** — Will be auto-detected from the uploaded file
4. Upload the **audio file**
5. Click **Save** or **Publish**

### Managing albums

1. Go to **Music > Albums**
2. Click **Add Album** to create a new album
3. Enter the **album title**, **artist**, **release year**, and **cover art**
4. Add songs to the album by selecting from existing songs or uploading new ones
5. Arrange the song order by dragging and dropping
6. Click **Save**

### Managing artists

1. Go to **Music > Artists**
2. Click **Add Artist** to create a new artist profile
3. Enter the **artist name**, **bio**, and upload a **photo** (optional)
4. Click **Save**

## Managing Playlists

Playlists allow you to curate collections of sermons and music for your congregation.

### Creating a featured playlist

1. Go to **Playlists** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Playlist**
3. Enter a **title** and **description** for the playlist
4. Add content by searching and selecting sermons or songs
5. Arrange the order by dragging and dropping items
6. Toggle **Featured** to make it appear prominently in the app
7. Click **Save**

### Editing playlist order

1. Open the playlist you want to edit
2. Drag and drop items to rearrange them
3. Click **Save** to update the order

### Removing items from a playlist

1. Open the playlist
2. Click the **Remove** button (X icon) next to the item you want to remove
3. Click **Save**

## Managing Literature

The Literature section allows you to manage books and written content available on the platform.

### Adding a book

1. Go to **Literature** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add Book**
3. Fill in the book details:
   - **Title** — The name of the book
   - **Author** — Select or add the author
   - **Description** — A brief summary or description
   - **Cover Image** — Upload a cover image
   - **Category** — Assign a category

5. Click **Save** or **Publish**

### Editing or removing literature

- To **edit**: Click on the book title and update any fields, then click **Save**
- To **unpublish**: Change the status to **Draft**
- To **delete**: Click **Delete** and confirm

## Analytics and Reports

The Analytics section provides insights into how your congregation uses the platform.

### Available reports

- **Overview** — Total users, active users, new sign-ups, and subscriber count
- **Streaming** — Most played sermons, total listening hours, peak usage times
- **Downloads** — Most downloaded content, total downloads, storage usage
- **Subscriptions** — Active subscribers, new subscriptions, cancellations, revenue summary
- **Engagement** — Average session duration, most active days, popular content categories

### Viewing reports

1. Go to **Analytics** in the sidebar
2. Select the report type from the tabs at the top
3. Use the **date range picker** to filter data by time period
4. Reports can be viewed as charts or tables
5. Click **Export** to download report data as a CSV file

## Push Notifications

Send push notifications to keep your congregation informed about new content, events, and announcements.

### Sending a notification

1. Go to **Notifications** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Notification**
3. Enter the **title** (short, attention-grabbing)
4. Enter the **message body** (keep it concise — under 200 characters works best)
5. Choose the **audience**:
   - **All users** — Send to everyone
   - **Subscribers only** — Send only to users with active subscriptions
   - **Custom segment** — Select specific groups if available
6. Optionally set a **deep link** — this determines where users go when they tap the notification (e.g., a specific sermon or announcement page)
7. Choose to **send immediately** or **schedule** for a future date and time
8. Review and click **Send**

::: tip
Use push notifications thoughtfully. Sending too many notifications can lead users to disable them. Aim for meaningful, timely messages.
:::

## Quote of the Day

The Quote of the Day feature displays an inspirational message to users when they open the app.

### Managing quotes

1. Go to **Quote of the Day** in the sidebar
2. You will see a calendar view or list of upcoming quotes
3. Click **Add Quote** to create a new one
4. Enter the **quote text** and the **author/source**
5. Select the **date** it should be displayed
6. Click **Save**

### Tips for quotes

- Prepare quotes in advance so there is always a fresh message
- Use scripture references, inspirational sayings, or passages from church leaders
- Keep quotes concise — they should be easy to read at a glance

## Weekly Sermon

The Weekly Sermon feature highlights one sermon on the app's home screen each week.

### Selecting the weekly sermon

1. Go to **Weekly Sermon** in the sidebar
2. Browse or search for the sermon you want to feature
3. Select it and choose the **week** it should be featured
4. Click **Save**

The selected sermon will appear prominently on the home screen during the chosen week, making it easy for members to find.

## Subscription Management Overview

While individual users manage their own subscriptions, administrators can view subscription data and assist with account-related issues.

### What administrators can see

- Total number of active subscribers
- Subscription plan distribution (which plans are most popular)
- Recent subscription changes (new, cancelled, expired)
- Revenue summaries and trends

### What administrators can do

- View a user's subscription status from their profile
- Direct users to [subscriptions.christgospel.org](https://subscriptions.christgospel.org) for self-service management
- Escalate billing issues to **support@christgospel.org**

::: info
For security reasons, administrators cannot view or modify payment details. All payment information is handled securely through Stripe.
:::

## Security Best Practices

As an administrator, you have access to sensitive data and management tools. Follow these practices to keep your account and the platform secure.

### Account security

- **Use a strong, unique password** — Avoid reusing passwords from other sites. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
- **Enable two-factor authentication** — This adds an extra layer of protection. Go to **Profile > Security** to set it up.
- **Save your backup codes** — When you set up 2FA, you will receive backup codes. Store them in a safe place in case you lose access to your verification method.
- **Do not share your credentials** — Each administrator should have their own account. Never share login details.

### Content management

- **Review before publishing** — Double-check sermon details, song information, and book metadata before making content live
- **Use scheduling** — When possible, schedule content in advance rather than publishing immediately, so you can review and catch any errors
- **Unpublish instead of deleting** — If you need to temporarily remove content, unpublish it rather than deleting. Deletion is permanent.

### User management

- **Limit admin access** — Only grant admin roles to people who need them
- **Review admin accounts regularly** — Remove access for team members who no longer need it
- **Monitor activity** — Check the Analytics section periodically for unusual activity

### General tips

- **Keep your browser up to date** — An updated browser helps protect against security vulnerabilities
- **Log out when finished** — Especially on shared or public computers
- **Report suspicious activity** — If you notice anything unusual, contact **support@christgospel.org** immediately
